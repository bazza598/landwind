## #1 — Histrips Onyx Sleep Watch (the chosen product) — 10/10

**The hottest signal in the dataset.** Wrist-worn vibration alarm for heavy sleepers; graduated
intensity, no audio, no partner disturbance.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Sell price | $34.95 |
| Est. COGS | $8–12 (vibration wristband, outsourced HW) |
| Gross margin | ~68–75% |
| EU spend (1 ad) | €40,306 (June 11) → ~€52.8K (June 13, same creative) |
| EU reach (1 ad) | 3.66M impressions |
| Active ads | 406 |
| 1W ad growth | +453% (just found their scaling creative) |

**Ad angle:** empathy-led ("you've struggled to wake up your whole life") → social proof → scarcity.

**Competitive gaps we exploit:**
- **No bundle** → "Heavy Sleeper Kit" (watch + mask + earplugs) lifts AOV ~$35→$65.
- **No subscription** (replacement bands / accessories).
- **No influencer/TikTok creator army** — organic upside.
- **No B2B** (hotels, hostels, shift workers, military).
- **No travel positioning** ("silent alarm for shared rooms/hostels") = viral hook.

→ This is the WakeWave thesis (docs 02–03): white-label the same hardware, own the *heavy
sleeper problem* story, win on brand/CX/bundle + clean compliant claims (the incumbent's
"99% / 10,000+ / save 50%" copy is an ASA liability in the UK).
