# The Wake-Up Routine Guide
### Included with your WakeWave Sleep Kit
*Set up your watch — and your mornings — in 10 minutes.*

## Part 1: Set up your WakeWave (2 minutes)
1. **Charge it fully** before the first night (cable in the box).
2. **Set your wake time** directly on the watch — no app, no account, no pairing.
3. **Choose your vibration profile.** Start one level below maximum. If you're a very deep
   sleeper, go straight to the top level for the first week, then dial down.
4. **Wear it snug, not tight.** The motor works best in contact with your wrist — one finger
   should fit under the strap.
5. **Test it once while awake** so the sensation is familiar, not alarming, the first morning.

## Part 2: The 5 rules of reliable mornings
**Rule 1 — One alarm, no snooze.** Every snooze fragments your last hour of sleep into junk
sleep. WakeWave builds in intensity instead — let it do the escalating, not your finger.

**Rule 2 — Same wake time, even weekends (±1 hour).** Your body wakes easiest at a consistent
time. The single biggest upgrade to mornings costs nothing.

**Rule 3 — Light within 5 minutes.** Open curtains or step outside. Morning light is the signal
that ends grogginess fastest.

**Rule 4 — Feet on the floor before the debate starts.** The moment you feel the vibration peak,
sit up and put both feet down. Don't negotiate with a half-asleep brain.

**Rule 5 — Phone stays out of reach until you're upright.** You bought a silent alarm partly to
stop the 2am-notification doom loop. Keep the phone across the room; the watch has the only job
that matters at 6am.

## Part 3: For couples & shift workers
- **Different wake times:** each WakeWave is fully independent — set yours, they set theirs,
  nobody hears either.
- **Rotating shifts:** reset the alarm when you set out tomorrow's clothes — anchor it to an
  existing habit so it never gets missed.
- **Nap mode:** short on sleep after nights? Set a 20–25 minute vibration nap before a shift —
  long enough to restore, short enough to avoid grogginess.

## Part 4: Care & troubleshooting

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Vibration feels weak | Tighten strap one notch; check charge level |
| Slept through it (rare) | Raise vibration level; move watch to your dominant wrist |
| Strap irritation | Swap to the spare strap; wash band weekly with mild soap |
| Battery draining fast | Lower vibration level; full recharge cycle |
