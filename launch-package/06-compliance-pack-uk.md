## Safe vs risky language

| ✅ Safe | ❌ Risky / banned |
|---|---|
| "Helps heavy sleepers wake up gently without disturbing their partner" | "99% success rate" (no substantiation) |
| "Vibration that builds until you wake" | "Clinically proven" / "doctor recommended" |
| "Battery lasts up to 7 days" (after sample test logged) | "Improves sleep quality / fixes insomnia" (medical claim) |
| "Rated ★4.6 by 112 customers" (live Judge.me figure) | "10,000+ tested" (invented) |
| "60-day returns" (policy published & honoured) | "Was £69.99, now £34.99" (no genuine prior price) |
| "Offer ends Sunday" (it genuinely ends) | Perpetual countdown timers (DMCCA fake urgency) |

## Why this is also strategy

Histrips runs "99% wake up on time", "97% energy improvement", "Tested by 10,000+", and rolling
"save 50%". Every one is an ASA complaint waiting to happen. If they enter the UK hard, their
copy is exposed; ours is bulletproof. Compliance here isn't a tax — it's the moat and the brand
voice ("Questions, answered honestly").

## Record-keeping rules
1. `evidence/` folder in this directory: every claim → its proof (sample test video, supplier
   cert, Judge.me export, screenshot, date).
2. WinningHunter JSON pulls retained.
3. Price-history log from day 1 — required before EVER running a "was/now" promotion
   (28-day genuine-price rule of thumb).
4. Keep every ad version + run dates (ASA investigations look backwards).
